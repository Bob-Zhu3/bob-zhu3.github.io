Whispers of Destiny
19 Feb 2022

Life and the path of life inexplicably intersect and run parallel,
Existence and the random tranquility of existence are steady yet extreme.
The vast sea that the little boat seeks has always been here,
It's just that in the past, there were rebellions and evasions, each self-justified.

There have been trembling, sharp sword strikes, and struggles with shattered chess pieces,
Also, through countless nights and experienced all four seasons.
Loki's promises become detestable,
In Aphrodite's whispers, I see the self I desperately want to express.

The boredom of restlessness cannot withstand the escape from reality,
Each search is just a straw raincoat in the downpour.
Turning back, unable to grasp the vividness of camellia blossoms,
Silence is just my response, my humble tribute.

Using time to dilute is not the foundation of problem-solving,
Respecting commitments is the premise for lasting bonds.
No need to question the opportune, no need to evade,
Confronting oneself is my ultimate suggestion.

I don't want to criticize cognitive deviations,
For even the author can't tell if this is delusion or true intent.
There are indeed differences between people,
At least I'm grateful for my decisiveness and pride.

Can we bid farewell to inconsistencies in words and actions?
I stand under the setting sun, gazing at the horizon and sighing:
When did arrogance and recklessness grow on my land?

Mashed Potatoes
14 Jan 2022

I am but a humble potato,
Yesterday, lying in the soil,
Bantering with earthworms,
Whistling a tune,
Teasing passing butterfly maidens.
On sunny days, I revel in carefree grass, dancing,
On rainy days, I howl in wild, disarray with unkempt hair.

But now, as I awaken from slumber,
Encased in a coarse burlap sack,
Squeezed among countless potatoes,
Each with a weary countenance, aloof and indifferent.

Like prisoners,
Lined up for the assault of water guns,
For the first time, I see clearly,
My own pockmarked and speckled existence.
Oh, how wretched I am.

Silence,
A thousand arrows piercing my heart,
I realize, I am but a lowly potato.

The Death of the Potsticker
3 Dec 2021

Why am I so heartbroken
When the potsticker dies?

It slipped suddenly
From between two black chopsticks
Its eyes filled with despair
In the end
The earth held its captive
I can feel the bone-chilling cold behind it
It lost its life
No longer the steaming vibrant potsticker
It is tainted by the soil

Why am I so heartbroken
When the potsticker dies?

I saw
Its eyes filled with despair
Perhaps blaming me
For not giving it a resting place sooner
Will it resent me
For leaving it so cold
In its moment of death

My heart, too, seems enveloped in coldness
Just when the potsticker dies
